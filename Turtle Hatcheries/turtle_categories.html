Categories

Loggerhead Turtles

A species of oceanic turtle found all around the world is the loggerhead sea turtle. It is a marine reptile from the Cheloniidae family. When fully grown, the average loggerhead's carapace measures about 90 cm.

Green Turtles

The second-largest of the seven sea turtle species, the green sea turtle is very widespread geographically, breeding in over 80 nations and living in temperate and tropical coastal waters in about 140 nations.

Leatherback Turtles

The leatherback turtle gets its name from the fact that, unlike other turtles, it has a soft shell rather than a hard one. The Atlantic and Pacific Oceans are both traversed by this species of sea turtle, which is also one of the most migratory.

Olive Ridley

This sea turtle's name is related to the shade of Olive green that may be seen on its shell. Of all sea turtles, they are now the most prevalent. Due to the fact that they only nest in a few number of locations, any disturbance to even one nest beach might have a significant impact on the entire population.

Hawksbills

The word "hawksbill" refers to a bird's short, pointed beak. On their shells, they feature a peculiar pattern of interlocking scales that give the edges a serrated appearance. Due to their unique colors and patterns, their shells are quite precious and sometimes referred to as "tortoiseshell" in marketplaces.
